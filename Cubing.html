How to Solve a Rubik's Cube in 4 Steps

Prerequisites

Firstly, we will talk about notation. The basic Rubik's Cube notations are R, R', L, L', F, F', U, U', D, D', B, B'. Any notation without the ' you will turn clockwise 90 degrees, otherwise turn counterclockwise 90 degrees.

  • R stands for right, which you turn the face on the right.
  • L stands for left, you turn the face on your left when you see this.
  • U stands for up, which you turn the top face.
  • D stands for down, which you turn the bottom face.
  • F stands for front, which you turn the front face.
  • B stands for back, which you turn the back face.
A wide turn is a turn which you turn the first 2 layers of the cube.
If there is a 2 after the notation, it means you have to turn it twice. This is defined by a lowercase letter being used instead of an uppercase one.
Headlights are 2 adjacent corner pieces that have the same colour facing the same side.
M stands for middle, where you moving the middle layer vertically.

Step 1: The White Cross

To make the cross, first find the white center. Then find the edge pieces (the one with 2 colours on them) that have white on them.
Then look at the second colour of the white edge and align it with the matching coloured center.
After that, turn your cube until the white face of the edge aligns with the white center. Repeat that with the other 3 white edges.

Step 2: F2L

F2L, or first 2 layers, is the step where you make the first 2 layers of a cube. First, Find corner pieces (pieces with 3 colours on them) that have white on them. Now move this piece to the top face if it is already. Use R, U, R', U' to do this.
Next, align the corner piece by turning the top face to which it's in between the centers of the 2 non white colours.
Finally, use the R, U, R', U' algorithm until the corner piece looks like it's in the right spot. Repeat this with the other 3 corners.

Step 3: OLL

OLL stands for Orient Last Layer. For the first three algorithms ignore the corner pieces. If the yellow face looks like a dot, use this algorithm: F R U R' U' F' f R U R' U' f'
If the yellow face looks like a straight line, turn it so it's horizontal to you, then do this algorithm: F R U R' U' F'
If the yellow face has an L shape on it, make it face the bottom right corner and do this algorithm: f R U R' U' f'
If the yellow face looks like a fish, do this algorithm after aligning it to face the bottom left: R U R' U R U2 R'
Repeat that algorithm until all the yellow faces face up. If the yellow face has a cross shape on it but doesn't have 2 headlights on either side, do the algorithm: R U2 R2 U' R2 U' R2 U2 R
If the yellow face has a cross shape with 2 headlights on either side, make one of the headlights face you and do this algorithm: R U R' U R U' R' U R U2 R'
If the yellow face looks like a T with a wide head without headlights, do this algorithm, making the head face to the right: r U R' U' r' F R F'
If the yellow face looks like a T with a wide head with headlights, do this algorithm, making the headlights face you: R2 D R' U2 R D' R' U2 R'

Step 4: PLL

PLL stands for Permutate Last Layer. These 2 algorithms are for changing it to be the next algorithms.

Description Algorithm
If there are exactly 1 pair of headlights, do this algorithm after making the headlights face left. R U R' U' R' F R2 U' R' U' R U R' F'
If there are no headlights, do this algorithm. F R U' R' U' R U R' F' R U R' U' R' F R F'
Now look at where you need to move your edges to solve the cube.
Description Algorithm
If it's moving them in a cross shape, do this. M2 U M2 U2 M2 U M2
If it's moving in a clockwise triangular shape, do this. R2 U R U R' U' R' U' R' U R'
If it's moving in a anticlockwise triangular shape, do this. R U' R U R U R U' R' U' R2
Otherwise, do this. M' U M2 U M2 U M' U2 M2
